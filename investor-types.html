The Quant

Balanced Aggression

You take risk, trust your own process, and stay logical.

  • Typical pattern: active decisions with measured risk discipline.
  • Best next step: keep model governance and stress testing strict.
  • Watch out: overconfidence from model backtests.

Active Conviction Investor

High Drive

You act quickly, take strong positions, and follow conviction.

  • Typical pattern: concentrated calls with emotional swings.
  • Best next step: enforce hard position sizing and cool-down rules.
  • Watch out: chasing recent winners too aggressively.

Tactical Trend Analyst

Adaptive

You follow trends and adjust often, but keep decisions data-based.

  • Typical pattern: tactical rotations without full emotional impulse.
  • Best next step: keep a long-term core and cap tactical turnover.
  • Watch out: high turnover reducing long-run compounding.

Aggressive Reactive Trader

High Volatility

You chase momentum fast, with high upside and high risk.

  • Typical pattern: trend-following plus emotional overreaction.
  • Best next step: strict drawdown limits and a stable passive core.
  • Watch out: rapid switches in and out of positions.

Conservative Researcher

Risk-Controlled

You prefer safety, do deep research, and move carefully.

  • Typical pattern: low-noise decisions and controlled drawdowns.
  • Best next step: avoid excessive caution in durable bull regimes.
  • Watch out: missing opportunities by waiting too long.

Defensive Active Allocator

Careful but Reactive

You want control and safety, but stress can change your plan.

  • Typical pattern: risk-aware decisions with occasional fear-driven pivots.
  • Best next step: automate allocations and rebalance on fixed schedules.
  • Watch out: panic de-risking near market bottoms.

Passive Rational Allocator

Compounding Focus

You prefer simple, long-term investing with steady discipline.

  • Typical pattern: low-friction behavior and disciplined patience.
  • Best next step: stay diversified, low-cost, and rules-based.
  • Watch out: becoming too passive when assumptions change.

Passive Emotional Allocator

Stability Seeking

You want stability and benefit from low-maintenance investing rules.

  • Typical pattern: stress-driven timing errors if too hands-on.
  • Best next step: automate contributions and reduce discretionary trading.
  • Watch out: stopping contributions during volatile periods.
